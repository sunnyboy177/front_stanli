Stan Lee was born in Manhattan, New York, USA, with the name of Stanley Martin Lieber. After graduating from high school in 1939, he was introduced to the “Timely Comics” company as an office assistant by his uncle, which was the predecessor of “Marvel Comics”.
In 1941, Stan Lee first participated in the creation of superhero comics. He gave himself a pseudonym—Stan Lee, firstly shown on the cover of the third part of the “Captain America” series, which opened the legend of his comic creation career.
In the late 1950s, Timely comics was on the brink of collapse, and Stan Lee ever considered quitting the field. At the suggestion of his wife in 1961, he and his partner cartoonist Jack Kirby co-created the first superhero combination in the history of Marvel— “Fantastic Four” quickly became popular after its release. This year, Timely Comics officially changed its name to Marvel comics.
Throughout the 1960s, Stan Lee continued to collaborate with cartoonists such as Jack Kirby and Steve Ditko, and successively launched Hulk, Iron Man, Spider-Man, Thor, Ant-man, Black Panthers, Doctor Strange and X-Men, leading Marvel Comics into the golden age.
Subsequently, in view of the popularity of Iron Man, Thor, Hulk and the existence of the “Justice League”, the formation of the League of Legends belonging to Marvel became imperative, therefore the Avengers came into being. And Stan Lee soon added Ant-Man, Wasp, Hawkeye, Quicksilver and Scarlet Witch. Of course, the most special role is—American captain who was awakened.
In 1972, Stan Lee officially became the publisher and chairman of Marvel Comics, where he was responsible for the development of Marvel’s film and television projects. Later, Stan Lee made cameo appearances in each Marvel, and became a “super bonus scene”. He traversed the various Marvel movie universes on the screen. In reality, he continues to explore a universe centered on superheroes traversing in the movie, television and comics circles...

Stan Lee got his star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame in 2011. In the same year, “Stan Lee Los Angeles Comic Con”, as the first multimedia pop culture event in Los Angeles, was launched by Stan Lee and his Pow! Entertainment jointly in 2011, which has now become the top three in the United States as a “pilgrimage” for animation fans, attracting more than 100,000 fans in 2017.
